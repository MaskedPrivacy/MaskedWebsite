What is Masked Privacy, and what is MASKED?

The Masked Privacy Group is a small (but growing) team of digital privacy advocates. Big tech companies and malicious web services are constantly tracking, spying, and profiling unknowing individuals to deliver them personalized advertisements, and even report whether someone's a "suspicious or illegal" user via information sharing between companies. Similarily in blockchain tech and DeFi, there are bots, whale watchers, and potentially unwanted people tracking transactions of particular addresses. This can put a 'large-bag' holder feeling stuck or intimidated to make a move, as any transfers they do could ring alarm bells.


This is where MASKED comes in: An Ethereum solution for keeping transactions safe through obfuscation and creative coding. Through a variety of creative ideas and implementation, a perfect privacy token can be achieved. MASKED's contract auto-magically shows no holders, no transfer events, and obfuscated transfer data. The contract also allows users to "hide and unhide" their balance from any service or user that attempts to check that user's balance. Additionally, users can generate a non-changing hash value which can be used to 'confuse' the data shown in transfer Tx's on block explorers - creating a fully obfuscated transfer process. MASKED is essentially a private form of Ethereum.

Governance and Voting Rights

MASKED tokens will be used in the future for decision-making via a voting mechanism. In-depth detail on how votes will be cast, weighted, and whether a user is allowed to vote multiple times is currently being decided. Contracts for voting will be carefully designed to suit our needs as they become apparent.


Our governance system will also have a deflationary system built-in. As each vote costs a certain number of tokens, these are sent to a 'Burn Vault'. We can improve our eco-system by using the burn vault as a 'Rewards Vault' for future staking of tokens, thus the total supply will not go down as the burned (or rather, stuck within a contract) re-enter circulation via staked rewards.


Voting will be used to gather opinions from holders about where the project should head, what apps should be built, along with team structural changes. Ideas that are brought for voting can be from either the development team or the community.

Contract Extensions and 'Prophets'

The MASKED token contract was built such that an 'Owner' can write new contracts that interact directly with MASKED. This provides scalability to the eco-system, for example: new privacy methods or other functions can be added as an Extension contract, then if users wish to interact with the extension contract with added functionalities they are free to do so.


The concept of Extensions on the contract can be tied in with our future goal of transferring off-chain data onto the chain. We've completed building desktop apps that can gather information then push it onto a new contract which tracks such data. This contract could of course be an extension, with variables related to the type of off-chain data you're pushing to the contract.
